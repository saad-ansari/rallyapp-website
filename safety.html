Child Safety Standards

Last updated: February 2026

Rally is committed to the safety and protection of all users, especially children. We have zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) content or behavior on our platform.

1. Age Requirements

Rally is designed for users aged 13 and older. We do not knowingly allow users under the age of 13 to create accounts or use our services. Users must confirm they meet the minimum age requirement during registration.

2. Prohibited Content and Behavior

The following content and behavior is strictly prohibited on Rally:

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) of any kind, including images, videos, or text
  • Sexual exploitation of minors in any form
  • Grooming behavior or attempts to solicit minors
  • Content that sexualizes children
  • Sharing or requesting content involving the abuse of children
  • Any activity that endangers the physical or psychological safety of minors

3. Detection and Prevention

We employ multiple measures to detect and prevent CSAE on our platform:

  • Content Moderation: User-uploaded images are reviewed for policy violations
  • User Reporting: In-app reporting tools allow users to flag concerning content or behavior immediately
  • Account Review: Suspicious accounts are investigated and removed when violations are found
  • Age Verification: Users must confirm they meet minimum age requirements

4. Reporting Child Safety Concerns

If you encounter any content or behavior that violates our child safety standards, please report it immediately:

  • In-App: Use the report function available on profiles and content
  • Email: safety@rallyapp.me
  • Emergency: If a child is in immediate danger, contact local law enforcement directly

All reports are treated with urgency and confidentiality. We review reports promptly and take appropriate action.

5. Enforcement Actions

When we identify violations of our child safety standards, we take immediate action:

  • Immediate removal of violating content
  • Permanent termination of the offending account
  • Preservation of evidence for law enforcement when required by law
  • Reporting to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and other relevant authorities as required
  • Cooperation with law enforcement investigations

6. Cooperation with Authorities

Rally cooperates fully with law enforcement agencies investigating child exploitation. We comply with all legal requirements to report CSAM to:

  • National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) in the United States
  • Internet Watch Foundation (IWF) in the United Kingdom
  • Relevant national authorities in other jurisdictions

7. Training and Awareness

Our team members who handle user reports and content moderation receive training on:

  • Identifying potential CSAE content and grooming behavior
  • Proper handling and reporting procedures
  • Legal obligations and compliance requirements
  • Victim-centered approaches to handling sensitive cases

8. Contact Information

For questions about our child safety standards or to report concerns:

  • Child Safety Team: safety@rallyapp.me
  • General Support: support@rallyapp.me

9. Policy Updates

We regularly review and update our child safety standards to address emerging threats and incorporate best practices. Significant changes will be communicated to users and reflected in the "Last updated" date above.
